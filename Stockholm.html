Stockholm


Stockholm is the capital of Sweden and is also the largest urban area in Scandinavia. It is known for many things such as being home to UNESCO World Heritage sites along with other fascinating and captivating places such as:


Drottningholm Palace


Take a day excursion to Drottningholm and immerse yourself in a historically rich environment. Drottningholm Palace is one of Stockholm's three World Heritage Sites and Sweden's best-preserved royal palace, built in the seventeenth century and serving as the royal family's permanent residence. Queen Hedvig Eleonora commissioned the architect Nicodemus Tessin the Elder to build the palace according to a French pattern. Since then, many royal figures have left their imprint on the palace. The palace has spectacular salons from the seventeenth, eighteenth, and nineteenth centuries, as well as a lovely park, a one-of-a-kind palace theater, and a Chinese Pavilion. Beginning in 1681, the massive Baroque garden was put out according to drawings by Nicodemus Tessin the Younger. The palace and park are mostly open to the public all year. Since 1981, the palace has been the permanent home of the current royal family. The royal family has exclusive use of the rooms in the palace's southern wing. Drottningholm was the first Swedish site to be added to the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1991.


Fotografiska


Fotografiska is one of the largest gathering places for modern photography around the world. Each year, four huge, one-of-a-kind shows and roughly 20 smaller exhibitions are presented. There is a bookstore, a café, and a photo gallery. One of Stockholm's nicest views may be seen in the café on the top floor. Because of its ecological gastronomy, Eat Guides 360° Action awarded Fotografiska's restaurant the best in Sweden in 2019.



Moderna Museet


Discover one of Europe's most important collections of art from the twentieth century to the present, including works by Picasso, Dali, Derkert, and Matisse. Moderna Museet is situated on the beautiful island of Skeppsholmen. Rafael Moneo, a Spanish architect, designed the structure. Moderna charges admission to temporary exhibitions, however the Moderna Museet Collection is free of charge.
