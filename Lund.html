Lund


Lund is a city in the southern Swedish province of Scania, across the Öresund strait from Copenhagen. The town had 91,940 inhabitants out of a municipal total of 121,510 as of 2018. It is the seat of Lund Municipality, Scania County. The Öresund Region, which includes Lund, is home to more than 4.1 million people.

Lund Cathedral


The bishop of Lund has his seat at Lund Cathedral. It was founded in 1080 as a Lutheran cathedral in the Romanesque style. Its 55-meter-high towers serve as a landmark throughout the city. The church's oldest bell was cast in 1513. It is made up of five organs, the largest of which dates from 1932 to 1934. Every year, over 700,000 people from all over the world, both Catholics and non-Catholics, come to see it.



Botanical Gardens (Botaniska Tradgarden)


Lund University owns and operates Botanical Gardens (Botaniska Tradgarden), a public botanical garden. It comprises 7000 plant kinds and covers an area of 8 hectares. Some of these are kept in greenhouses in the climate zones where they belong. In 1974, it was designated as a national historic site. It is available to everyone and there is no entry fee. This is a must-see attraction for anyone visiting the city.


Museum Of Cultural History, Kulturen


Kulturen (Museum of Cultural History) is Sweden's second-oldest open-air museum. Georg Karlin, a local historian, founded the museum in 1892. The museum is organized into two sections: an open-air museum and a traditional museum. It is located near the Lund Cathedral. The museum has a one-of-a-kind collection of exhibits, such as historic jewelry from the historical region of Scania. Overall, it's a fascinating location steeped in history.
